Portfolio design

  • Inception: 2026-07-13. Starting notional: $1,000,000. Positions: 28 — diversified for lower single-deal risk.
  • Universe: pending public-target deals with expectedClose ≤ 18 months and closingProbability ≥ 60%.
  • Spread filter: 5 bp–30%. Wider than 30% is treated as a broken-deal signal that a simple screen cannot reliably price.
  • Expected return: E[R] = p · spread + (1 − p) · (−15%). The 15% assumed downside is the average public-to-public deal break historically.
  • Ranking: Sharpe-like score, E[R] ÷ standard deviation of the two-outcome distribution.
  • Weighting: proportional to max(E[R], 0) × closingProbability, capped 8% and floored 1.5%. High-Sharpe positions (top 9 names) hit the 8% cap; small-spread tail names are held at the 1.5% floor as diversifiers — the tail alone would not be a positive-expectancy trade in isolation but reduces concentration risk in the book.
  • Currency: USD. Foreign-listed tickers (e.g. RFX.L) use local price; no FX conversion.
  • Refresh: current prices refreshed daily via the 7 AM ET deal-scan pipeline. Position entry prices are frozen at inception.
  • Model portfolio for illustration only — not investment advice, not a real trade book.
