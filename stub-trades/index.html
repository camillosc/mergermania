Deal mechanics & key disclosures

Per the Amended & Restated SpaceX License Purchase Agreement (SATS Q1'26 10-Q Note 10, dated Nov 5 2025): SATS sells its full AWS-4 / H-Block / AWS-3 spectrum portfolio to SpaceX for approximately $20B total consideration. The 10-Q references "up to $11 billion paid in SpaceX's Class A common stock at $212 per share" — but per SpaceX's own IPO prospectus cited by Barron's, the actual deliverable post-split is ~262M shares, implying a ~5.05× SpaceX stock split between the SPLA signing and the SPCX IPO. The Spectrum Transfer Closing is expected H1 2026; the Spectrum Acquisition Closing — when shares are actually delivered to SATS — is on or about November 30, 2027. At that closing, $9.821B of SATS' Seller Notes (10¾% due 2029, 6¾% due 2030, 3⅞% convertibles due 2030) are extinguished.

SATS basic shares: 289.8M (Class A 158.47M + Class B 131.35M, per Q1'26 10-Q cover, Apr 30 2026). Barron's references "equity market value $39B assuming conversion into stock of a convertible bond deal" — at $113.52 (Barron's quote) this implies fully-diluted ~343.6M shares, which is what the page uses for per-share math.

The Charlie Ergen factor. Barron's frames the structural overhang: Ergen is chairman, CEO and controlling shareholder; "an industry maverick" with unclear long-term plans. EchoStar declined to hold a conference call after its May earnings release — investors have no recent narrative update. At 73, a sale to SpaceX would offer a clean exit; meanwhile the $23B AT&T spectrum sale (separate deal) deleverages the parent ahead of the SpaceX share delivery.
