Deal mechanics & key disclosures

Per the Amended & Restated SpaceX License Purchase Agreement dated Nov 5 2025 (Q1'26 10-Q Note 10): SATS will sell its full AWS-4 / H-Block / AWS-3 spectrum portfolio to SpaceX for approximately $20B total. Of that, up to $11B is paid in SpaceX Class A common stock at $212/share — implying 51.886M SpaceX shares. The Spectrum Transfer Closing is expected H1 2026; the Spectrum Acquisition Closing — when the SpaceX shares are actually delivered to SATS — is expected on or about November 30, 2027. At the Spectrum Acquisition Closing, the deal extinguishes $9.821B of SATS' Seller Notes (10¾% due 2029, 6¾% due 2030, 3⅞% convertibles due 2030), so the post-deal balance sheet is materially less levered than today's.

SATS' Class A common stock outstanding was 158,465,020 and Class B was 131,348,468 as of April 30, 2026 (Q1'26 10-Q cover page) — total ~289.8M shares. SPCX IPO'd on Nasdaq today (6/12/26), priced at $135, and closed day 1 at $160.95 (+19.2% from IPO), implying SpaceX market cap ~$2.11T. The SPCX mark is currently $51/share below the $212 contract basis at which SATS will receive its shares — so on a mark-to-market basis the stake is a $2.65B paper loss (no tax burden in this scenario; possibly a deferred tax asset if marked).

Strategic angle: SATS positioned as Starlink Direct-to-Cell partner via Boost Mobile and Hughes — long-term commercial agreement included in the SPLA gives Boost subscribers access to next-gen Starlink Direct to Cell service. Excluded from RemainCo: AWS-3 re-auction exposure (max payment up to $2.921B per SATS Q1'26 10-Q Auction 113 disclosure), and unresolved FCC matters. The $85 RemainCo is a user-provided point estimate; sensitivity to it is large (every $5/sh of RemainCo moves SOTP by the same $5/sh).

METHODOLOGY & CAVEATS

Primary source = SEC. Stake size (51.886M SpaceX shares, $11B at $212/sh issue price) and SATS share counts (158.47M Class A + 131.35M Class B = 289.8M total) verified against SATS Q1 2026 10-Q (Note 10 "Recent Developments — SpaceX License Purchase Agreement" + cover page). Where third-party writeups (Fortune, Barron's) and SEC filings differ, SEC prevails.

SPCX live = authoritative SpaceX mark. Prior secondary tender ($800B Dec 2025) and IPO pricing ($1.77T at $135) retained as historical comparators only — all current discount/premium math uses live SPCX close from Nasdaq via Yahoo Finance.

Tax burden. At SPCX $160.95 vs $212 contract basis, mark-to-market shows a $2.65B paper loss — no tax burden in the immediate-sale scenario. Upside scenarios (SPCX above $212) would trigger 21% US federal corporate tax on (mark − $11B basis); in practice these DTLs are routinely permanent and rarely realized at parent level.

RemainCo = user-provided. $85/sh for Hughes Network + Boost Mobile + retained spectrum is a point estimate, not an SEC-derived value. Sensitivity is 1:1 with the assumption.

Closing timing matters. SATS does not yet hold SpaceX equity. The Spectrum Acquisition Closing is expected on or about Nov 30, 2027. Until then, the stake is a contractual right contingent on regulatory approvals (FCC + DOJ).
