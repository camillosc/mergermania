METHODOLOGY & CAVEATS

Stake valuation. Public stakes marked at last close. Private stakes (SpaceX) marked to last confirmed secondary-market tender — Dec 2025 at $421/sh, implying ~$800B valuation. Forward-looking IPO targets ($1.5T-$1.77T per roadshow press) are not used as the basis; they are noted in the catalyst column as upside scenarios only. Per-share allocations use latest 10-Q/10-K share counts.

Tax burden. Computed as (mark − basis) × statutory corporate rate, assuming a hypothetical immediate-sale scenario. US parents use 21% federal rate. SoftBank uses 30.62% Japan effective corporate rate. Prosus uses 25.8% Netherlands. In practice these deferred tax liabilities are rarely realized at parent level — long-tail DTLs are routinely permanent items. After-tax discount is therefore conservative; pre-tax discount is the relevant figure for most realistic outcomes (in-kind distribution, exchange, holding indefinitely).

Holdco friction. Even after netting tax, Malone-style holdcos and dual-class trackers historically trade at structural discounts of 10-20% due to: control overhang, lack of catalysts, dual-class governance, and tracker-share share-class complexity. The catalyst column identifies known triggers that have historically narrowed these gaps (forced split-offs, take-privates, spin-offs, mergers).

Look-through ≠ liquidation. The numbers shown are mark-to-market exposures, not realizable prices. A 30% NAV discount on a holdco is not free money — it's compensation for governance, tax overhang, and timing risk.
